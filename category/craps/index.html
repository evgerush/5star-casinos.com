Getting to Know Craps Betting System

Craps is considered to be one of the most exciting casino games, mainly due to the fast game execution. One or more players can win on just a single dice roll, and can also lose in that instant. It all depends on how lucky they are. Another thing that adds to the excitement of craps is the numerous ways on how to bet your money.

Craps is played by rolling a dice. By the way, for those who don’t have an idea about it, a die (singular of dice) is a cube shaped object (often made of wood or plastic) with dots engraved on all 6 sides: each side represents the numbers 1 to 6 by the count of dots, ie, one dot represents number 1, two dots is 2, etc. When the dice is rolled, the numbers on the sides of the two dice which are facing top are added and termed as the dice outcome. The shooter of the dice can either win or lose depending on the following situations:

– Natural: If the shooter rolls the dice and the first dice outcome, which is called the come out roll, is a number 7 or 11, then he wins the game.

– Craps: If the come out roll is either 2, 3, or 12, then the shooter loses, but still rolls the dice.

– Point: If any number other than the already mentioned is the come out roll, then a point is established, and the shooter must roll the same number before a number 7 in order to win.

– 7-out: If a point is established and number 7 is rolled first before the point number is rolled

Depending on the dice outcomes, casinos offer a very wide variety of betting rules where players can place their bets. Online casino craps also provides online gamers with more or less the same number of craps betting variants. The shooter of the dice is mainly the center of attraction in playing craps. Players can place their bet on two main betting categories: line bets and side bets.

Line bets are the most fundamental and basic bets in the game. This type of bet is resolved with reference to the winning or losing situation of the dice shooter.

1. Pass Line bet is the bet made by the players where they root on the dice shooter. If the dice shooter wins, then the pass line bet also wins. The bet loses on Craps and 7-out, together with the shooter.

2. Don’t Pass Line bet is the opposite: if the shooter lose either on Craps (except number12) or 7-out, then the Don’t Pass Line bet win. If the shooter’s come out roll is 12, then it is considered as draw. The bet loses on Natural and if the shooter obtains the point number first before a 7.

3. Come bet is made after a point is established, and it applies the same rules as the Pass Line bet. However, if the shooter rolled a 7 upon placing the Come bet, then the come bet wins, but the shooter loses, together with the Pass Line and Come bets made before it.

4. Don’t Come bet is the opposite of Come bet, and applies the same rules as the Don’t Pass Line Bet.

Odds bets are bets made to reinforce the Pass Line and Don’t Pass Line bets, as well as the Come and Don’t Come bets.

Side bets, also called Proposition bets, are bets on craps that are based on the outcomes of the dice rolls. There are three types of side bets, namely, the Single-roll bets, Multi-roll bets, and the Player bet.

Single-roll bets are those that are resolved on a single dice roll.
- Aces or Snake Eyes – bet that number 2 will be rolled
- Boxcars, Cornrows, or Midnight – bet for number 12
- Ace-Deuce – bet for number 3
- Yo – bet for number 11
- Hi-Lo – bet that either number 2 or 12 will be rolled
- Any Craps or Three-Way – bet for either 2, 3, or 12
- Any Seven – bet for number 7
- C & E – combined bet for both Any Craps and Yo (11), where only one of the two bets may win
- Field bet – bet where the player wins on either 3, 4, 9, 10, or 11, and wins double on 2 and 12, but loses if the dice outcome is either 5, 6, 7, or 8
- Horn bet – a bet for each of the numbers 2, 3, 11, and 12 at the same time, which results to four separate bets where only one of the bets may win
- Whirl bet or World bet – the Horn bet + Any Seven bet, resulting to a five-unit bet for each of the numbers of the Horn and the number 7. If number 7 wins, it is a draw or push, because the bets lost on the Horn is won in the number 7.
- On the Hop – bet on any resulting combination of the come out roll, such as 3-5, 4-2, 3-3, etc. A player can choose whether to bet on double numbers (2-2, 3-3, etc.) or different numbers (3-5, 2-3, etc.)

Multi-roll bets are bets that are resolved by one or more dice rolls.
- Place bet – a bet on any of the point numbers (4, 5, 6, 8, 9, and 10) to be rolled before a 7
- Buy bet – a bet similar to the Place bet, butwith better payouts for a 5% commision charge if it wins
- Lay bet – a bet on any of the point numbers (4, 5, 6, 8, 9, and 10), in which it wins if number 7 is rolled first before the number, and is charged a 5% commision
- Big 6 – a bet on number 6 to be rolled before 7
- Big 8 – a bet on number 8 to be rolled before 7
- Hard Way – a bet on one of the numbers 4, 6, 8, or 10 to be rolled, with the dice showing similar numbers, before the number 7
- Easy Way – bet for one of the numbers 4, 6, 8, or 10 to be rolled, with the dice showing different numbers, before number 7

A Player bet is a bet placed on the dice shooters. The Fire bet is one type of player bet, where the players can bet on one or more point numbers to be obtained and won by the shooter in more than three times in a row.
